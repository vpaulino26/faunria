# The Pools

# Main Inhabitants

Raccoons, Rabbits, Squirrels, Frogs

# Geography

**Wet deciduous woodland with vernal pools and shrub-thicket clearings.**  

Vernal pools and damp lowlands support frogs, while rabbits and squirrels use the mix of understory thickets and mast trees; raccoons do well in the same water-rich forest.

# Capital

**Vernpool:** a low, sprawling capital on a gentle rise among a constellation of vernal pools. The “spring-road” is a seasonal causeway network, frog sanctuaries are protected pools, and rabbit and squirrel districts weave through hazel thickets and mast orchards.

## Capital Design

### Descriptive features

- Is a giant castle surrounded by vernal pools
- squirrels live on the surrounding trees, has rope bridges to lead down to the castle
- raccoons control the ports and bridges in and out of the pool
- destination vacation for frogs
- Due to foliage, rabbits have started to eat nuts here

# Lore

A city only possible through the Architect’s invention of bridges. With rope bridges from sky-to-wall and wood bridges in-and-out of the pond, this fruitful city would not have been achievable without the architects.
